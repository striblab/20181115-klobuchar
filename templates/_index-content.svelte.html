Klobuchar's national profile has risen in part on her ability to win votes in Republican-leaning areas of Minnesota.

Consider that about 3,000 of Minnesota's 4,120 election precincts voted for Donald Trump in 2016.

Red areas represent precincts that voted for Donald Trump in 2016. Areas that voted for Hillary Clinton are not highlighted.

This year, Klobuchar won about 1,250 of those — including traditionally conservative areas in rural Minnesota and exurban Republican enclaves like St. Cloud.

By contrast, Democratic Governor-elect Tim Walz won fewer than 500 Trump precincts — including swaths of the Iron Range and southern Minnesota, where he served as a congressman for 12 years.

And with few exceptions, Sen. Tina Smith, Klobuchar's Democratic colleague in the Senate, won many of the same Trump precincts Walz did.

In many areas, voters even split their tickets, supporting Klobuchar while also voting for Smith's and Walz's Republican opponents.

Although she faced a relatively unknown and poorly financed opponent, Klobuchar's 24-point victory this year shows a broad base of urban, suburban and rural support that Democrats are hoping to tap into in 2020.

Blue areas represent precincts that voted for Klobuchar in 2020. Red areas voted for her Republican opponent, Jim Newberger.

Source: Election results from the Minnesota Secretary of State; precinct data from Minnesota Geospatial Commons. Maps do not include comparison data for a small number of precincts that changed between 2016 and 2018.
